Crusing Day 1: Valencia

Since it was an early morning we had room service for breakfast – bagels, fruit, and Diet Coke.  Our shore excursion for the day was called "Best of Valencia".

Our tour guide allowed for some free time because some in our group had to use the restroom (including Mom).  We were told it was in the rear of the city's market, where the fresh fish was located.  My acute sense of smell almost got the best of me.  I was running through the market, gagging and asking for the bathroom, while trying not to vomit.

After a walking tour of the city, we visited the Valencia Cathedral.  Encased in glass on the altar was the chalice used in the Last Supper, also known as the Holy Grail.  When we returned to the ship, we ordered room service for lunch.

At 5:30, we went to the captain's reception. We saw Jacques Pepin, a renowned French chef who cooked with Julia Child at one point in his career.  As he was walking in, Mom stopped him and asked if he would take a picture with me.  I was so embarrassed but now that I look back on it I'm grateful she did it.

We ate at the Grand Dining Room and had a fantastic dinner.  Mom ate prime rib, and I had lobster.
