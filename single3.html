Touring Barcelona: Day 1

Our daylong tour of Parc Güell and Bacílica de la Sagrada Família began at Parc Güell.  This is an outdoor area that Antoni Gaudí built and it was amazing!  There were fountains and landscapes built into and around the structures.  It is a site that cannot adequately be described and definitely can't be missed.

Our next stop was la Sagrada Família.  The construction began in 1885 and it is still under construction.  Entering the basilica, the façade's theme is The Nativity.  Gaudí told the story of Jesus' birth, the shepherds, and the wise men.  On the opposite side, the façade's theme is the Passion.  The Nativity façade is very different from the Passion façade.  The Nativity exterior has ornate detail and Gaudí wanted this entrance to be the most attractive and welcoming to the public.  The Passion exterior is constructed in a cubist style.  Construction began on the final façade, the Glory or Resurrection, in 2002.  Gaudí designed the inside of the basilica to capture and elevate the experience with light and color with many stained glass windows.

The whole project was financed without help from the Catholic Church, as Gaudí did not want the restrictions that were going to be placed on him.  To raise some capital, the project rented out land around the building, knowing the architects would not use the land for quite a while.  Houses were constructed, and laws in Spain were altered.  If you resided on a property for 50 years, you could not be evicted.  Currently, there are lawsuits to reclaim the land to finish construction.  Since Gaudí died in 1926, seven different site managers or architects have taken over the project to finish Gaudí's design.

We ended the day with tapas and sangria for dinner.
